My Favorite Fajitas

Mexican-style food is one of my favorite cuisines, which is why I want to share two of my favorite fajita recipes with you.

Creamy Chicken Fajitas

Ingredients

  • 1.5 lb of boneless skiless chicken breasts
  • 2 bell peppers, any color, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 8 oz cream cheese, cubed
  • 1/2 cup salsa
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • Any rice of your choosing

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 400°F. Spray the bottom of a casserole dish with a little bit of oil.
  2. In a large bowl, mix the chicken with all of the seasonings: garlic, paprika, cumin, salt, ground black pepper. (Pro tip: if the chicken seems thick, butterfly it into 4 smaller pieces before seasoning)
  3. lace your seasoned chicken on the bottom of the casserole dish. Arrange the sliced vegetables around the chicken in the dish, avoiding covering the top of the chicken. This may require some adjusting of the chicken.
  4. Scatter the salsa and cubed cream cheese across the top of the dish.
  5. Cook for 30 minutes. While the fajitas are in the oven, prepare your rice base as needed.
  6. Remove from the oven and use two fork to shred the chicken to your desired size. If your chicken needs more time, put it back in the oven for 5 minute increments until ready.
  7. Once cooked and shredded to your desired size, serve warm over a bed of rice.

This recipe takes little to no effort. The hardest part is waiting for it to cook! I like to serve this over a bed of white rice to soak of the extra, creamy sauce. I find this meal filling, and a great option for weekly meal prepping. It lasts me anywhere from 4-6 meals and can be easily reheated in the microwave.


Shrimp Fajitas

Ingredients

  • 1 lb of shrimp, peeled, and deveined
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 2 bell peppers, sliced
  • 1/4 cup of olive oil
  • 1 lime

Seasoning

  • 1/2 tablespoon chili powder
  • 1/2 tablespoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon pepper

Toppings (all optional)

  • Avocado
  • Sour Cream
  • Salsa
  • Corn
  • Beans
  • Feta
  • Rice
  • Tortillas

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, mix together all of the fajita seasonings
  2. In a large bowl, add the shrimp, two tablespoons of olive oil, the juice from half a lime, and the fajita seasoning. Toss until the shrimp is well coated and set it aside to marinate.
  3. Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in large pan over medium heat. Add the sliced bell peppers and onions and sauté until softened. Transfer cooked vegetables to a plate off the heat.
  4. In the same pan, add the shrimp and cook for about 2-3 minutes on each side or until they turn pink.
  5. Add the bell peppers and onions back into the skillet, mix everything together, and squeeze more lime juice on top. Serve in tortillas or as a bowl with any of your favorite toppings

I’m a huge fan of this recipe because it’s relatively easy, requires minimal work, it’s customizable, and it’s absolutely delicious. You could absolutely assemble these however you like, but here is what I do with mine:
I love to make a bowl with mine. I use a small base of white rice, add my shrimp fajitas and veggies, and top with ¼ can of corn, ¼ can of black beans, a small dallop of plain non-fat Greek yogurt (a great healthy substitute for sour cream!), ½ an avocado, and a sprinkle of feta. I know this may seem like a strange arrangement, but don’t knock it until you try it! I find this helps get some added nutrients and fiber into my diet to help keep me full longer.
This recipe lasts me for four meals. I store the fajita mixture in one reusable container, and my leftover corn and beans in a separate container. The fajitas should be eaten within four days, and they can be reheated in the microwave for about a minute, or until warmed all the way through.
